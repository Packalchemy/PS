If you work in FMCG, food and beverage, pharmaceuticals, or retail — the packaging industry's numbers in 2026 are impossible to ignore. The global packaging market, valued at $1.28 trillion in 2025, is on a trajectory to reach $1.75 trillion by 2035, according to Towards Packaging. That is not a niche industry. That is one of the largest manufacturing sectors on the planet — and it is changing faster than at any point in its history.

For brand managers, packaging engineers, procurement teams, and FMCG executives, understanding where this industry is headed is no longer optional. Sustainability regulations, shifting consumer expectations, geopolitical supply chain pressures, and the rise of smart packaging technology are all converging at once — creating both significant risk and enormous opportunity for brands that pay attention.

01 · The Global Picture

A Market Worth More Than the GDP of Most Countries

To put $1.28 trillion in perspective — the global packaging market is larger than the GDP of countries like Argentina, Sweden, or Poland. It touches every product category, every geography, and every consumer demographic on earth. And it is growing steadily, at a compound annual growth rate of 3.16% through 2035, according to Towards Packaging's March 2026 analysis.

$1.75T
The projected size of the global packaging market by 2035, up from $1.28 trillion in 2025 — representing cumulative growth of over $470 billion in a single decade.

Asia Pacific is the engine driving this growth. The region accounted for 38.80% of global packaging market share in 2025, according to Fortune Business Insights — and it is not slowing down. The combination of a rapidly expanding middle class, urbanisation, growing e-commerce volumes, and increasing demand for packaged food and consumer goods has made Asia Pacific the undisputed centre of gravity for global packaging demand.

Within Asia Pacific, three markets stand out above all others. China's packaging market is projected to reach $158.42 billion in 2026. India's market is projected to reach $110.66 billion in the same period. And Japan contributes another $79.65 billion, according to Fortune Business Insights. Together, these three markets represent approximately 30.5% of all global packaging consumption.

02 · India's Packaging Boom

India: The World's Most Exciting Packaging Market in 2026

India's total packaging market was valued at $101.12 billion in 2025 and is forecast to reach $169.73 billion by 2030 — advancing at a remarkable CAGR of 10.73%, according to BW CFO World's December 2025 industry analysis. This is more than triple the global packaging market's average growth rate.

Rising Disposable Incomes and Premiumisation

As incomes rise across both urban and rural India, consumers are trading up from unpackaged or loosely packaged goods to branded, well-packaged products. This shift toward branded, packaged goods is creating sustained demand for better, more sophisticated packaging solutions across every FMCG category.

E-Commerce and Quick Commerce

With India's e-commerce sector projected to hit $18.8 billion by 2030, the demand for packaging that can survive last-mile delivery is surging. Brands are rethinking packaging from a "shelf appeal" model to a "courier-proof" model — requiring right-sized packaging, frustration-free design, and structural integrity that holds up across India's varied delivery conditions.

Flexible Packaging Dominates

Within India's packaging mix, flexible packaging is the dominant format, holding approximately 65% of the consumer packaging market in 2025, according to Future Market Insights. This reflects the practical realities of India's market — flexible packaging is lighter, more cost-efficient, and better suited to the wide range of products sold across India's diverse retail landscape.

03 · Sustainability Mandates

The Regulatory Reality: EPR Rules Are Now in Full Force

Perhaps the most significant development in India's packaging industry in 2026 is the full enforcement of Extended Producer Responsibility regulations. From April 1, 2026, new EPR rules came into full effect, mandating a 70% recycling target for all packaging materials. This is not a future aspiration — it is current law.

⚑ Regulatory Checklist for FMCG Brands in India 2026

What Your Packaging Must Now Comply With

  • 70% recycling target for all packaging materials under new EPR rules (effective April 1, 2026)
  • Mandatory barcode or QR code on packaging from July 2025 for traceability and compliance reporting
  • Clear disclosure and reporting of pre-consumer waste quantities
  • Certification requirements for biodegradable plastics under PWM 2024 Amendment Rules
  • Recyclable content targets increasing annually — brands must track and report recycled content percentages

India's Bio-Packaging Revolution

One of the most exciting developments in response to these regulations is the rapid growth of India's bio-packaging sector. According to the India Brand Equity Foundation, the market size of bioplastic products was estimated at ₹4,069 crore for FY25 — and is expected to grow to ₹32,500 crore by FY35, representing a growth rate of approximately 23% annually.

04 · Geopolitical Pressures

The Headwinds: Tariffs, Trade Disruptions, and Cost Pressure

India's packaging industry faced significant headwinds in 2025 that will continue to shape strategy in 2026. However, the industry's growth story comes with its share of challenges. US tariffs of 50% on several categories of Indian exports created immediate pressure for packaging manufacturers with American market exposure. Export volumes for some film categories — particularly BOPP and BOPET films — reportedly declined by 15–25%, according to BW CFO World's analysis citing Cosmo First Group CFO Neeraj Jain.

The silver lining is significant: the industry's strategic rebalancing — renegotiating contracts, front-loading shipments, and diversifying into Europe and Southeast Asia — has proven resilient. With a new US-India trade deal reducing tariffs from 50% to 18% effective February 2026, the recovery window is now open.

05 · Outlook

What This All Means for FMCG Brands in 2026

The packaging industry in 2026 is not a background function — it is a strategic battleground. India, in particular, occupies a unique position. With a domestic market growing at over 10% annually, a young and increasingly brand-conscious consumer base, a rapidly maturing e-commerce sector, and a government pushing hard on sustainability compliance, Indian FMCG brands and their packaging partners face a decade of extraordinary opportunity.

★ Key Takeaways for FMCG & Packaging Professionals

  • The global packaging market is $1.28 trillion and growing — understanding its dynamics is essential for any FMCG professional.
  • India is growing at 10.73% CAGR — nearly three times the global average. The domestic opportunity is exceptional.
  • EPR rules are now law in India — the 70% recycling mandate effective April 1, 2026 is not optional.
  • Flexible packaging dominates India at 65% share — any brand making packaging decisions in India needs to understand this ecosystem deeply.
  • Bio-packaging is moving from niche to mainstream — with 23% annual growth projected to 2035, bio-based alternatives are becoming commercially viable at scale.
  • Smart packaging is now legally required — QR codes are mandatory from July 2025, and consumer engagement expectations make digital packaging essential.
  • US-India tariff deal creates recovery window — the February 2026 trade deal reducing tariffs to 18% opens a significant export opportunity for Indian packaging manufacturers.
