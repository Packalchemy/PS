★ Brand Strategies in Packaging

How India's Biggest FMCG Brands are Rethinking the Outer Layer

Packaging is no longer just protective — it is the primary channel for brand identity, sustainability narrative, and consumer trust. Four Indian FMCG giants show how it is done.

Read Full Report →
Dabur
Strategy: Carton-Free Sustainability

Dabur removed outer paper cartons from its Dabur Red toothpaste range — saving thousands of tonnes of paper annually. The saved paper is repurposed to create notebooks for underprivileged children, turning a packaging decision into a powerful social story.

Amul
Strategy: Mascot-Led Brand Recall

Amul's unchanged mascot and colour scheme across 60+ years is one of India's greatest brand assets. By keeping packaging identity locked while expanding SKUs, Amul has built instant recognition across 1 billion consumers — proof that consistency beats reinvention.

Marico
Strategy: Circular Packaging Commitment

Marico redesigned its Parachute coconut oil bottle with a flat-bottomed structure, fitting 10–15% more bottles per pallet and cutting transit losses. The company has committed to 100% recyclable packaging and 30% recycled content across its portfolio under India's EPR rules.

Parle
Strategy: Sachet Economy at Scale

Parle's mastery of the ₹5 and ₹10 pack format has made its biscuits accessible to rural India's 800 million consumers. Packaging at this price point requires ruthless material efficiency — Parle has turned that constraint into a distribution superpower.

The EPR Revolution: Why 2026 is the Year Indian Brands Can No Longer Ignore Packaging Waste Policy

From April 1, 2026, new EPR rules came into full effect in India, mandating a 70% recycling target for all packaging materials. For the first time, a coherent legislative environment is forcing brands to internalize the true cost of the packaging they put into the world — and the innovators who moved early are discovering that circularity is not just a compliance exercise, but a competitive advantage.
